Tsunami events¶

The major areas of the world that experience strong activity with potential for tsunami are along the boundaries of the Pacific ocean with other regions. However, in general, tsunamis can be generated in any coastal area, including inland seas and large lakes. TsunamiWorld, sourced from NOAA, maps some of the trunami-prone regions in the world.

Tsunami map across the world and color coded to the destructiveness of the earthquake and tsunami (Source: NOAA, Natural Hazards Viewer)¶

There are good records for recent tsunamis. However, for those in the past centuries, these are mostly based on the accounts of personal observations. TsunamiEvents shows the tsunami events caused due to earthquakes/volcanic eruptions/landslides/other causes between 1610 B.C. to 2017 A.D. The events are classified based on magnitude and cause by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA).

Map of tsunami events between 1610 B.C. to 2017 A.D. and color coded to the magnitude and cause of the event (Source: NOAA report on tsunami events)¶

The tabular data shown in TsunamiRegion, compiled by NOAA’s National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) under the UNESCO/IOC-NOAA partnership, provides a glossery of tsunami events over the last 2000 years. Tsunamis that have a travel time of 1-3 hours are classified as regional tsunamis and major ones over the last 2000 years are shown in TsunamiRegion.

List of tsunami events over the last 2000 years. The table also classifies the number of deaths and source of tsunami (Source: NOAA report on tsunamis)¶

The destruction caused by a tsunami can range to much larger extents. The tsunami that cause damage in regions beyond three hour of travel are classified as distant/teletsunami. Some of these from the last 300 years are shown in TsunamiDistant

List of teletsunami events over the last 300 years. The table also shows the source and distant locations where damage was caused (Source: NOAA report)¶

Tsunamis can be caused due to a variety of events. While majority of them are caused due to earthquakes, they also result due to volcanic eruptions, landslides and other events. These are shown in TsunamiSource and it is evident that most of these events are also in neighbourhood of the Pacific ocean.

Classification of source and mechanism of tsunami events. (Source: NOAA report on tsunami)¶
