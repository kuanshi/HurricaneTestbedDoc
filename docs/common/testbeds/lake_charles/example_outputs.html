3.8. Example Outputs¶

3.8.1. Running Testbed¶

R2D Interface¶

One approach to run the testbed simulation is via the R2D application After successfully download and launch, the major steps for setting up the run are listed as follows:

  1. Setting the Units in the GI panel as shown in Fig. 3.8.1.1 and checking interested output files.

    Fig. 3.8.1.1 R2D GI setup.¶

  2. Downloading and unzipping the HurricaneLaura_MappedPWS. Setting the Event File Listing Wind Field in the HAZ panel to the “EventGrid.csv” in the unzipped “IMs” folder. The app would automatically load the directory (Fig. 3.8.1.2).

    Fig. 3.8.1.2 R2D HAZ setup.¶

  3. Downloading the BIM_LakeCharles_Full.csv. Selecting CSV to BIM in the ASD panel and setting the Import Path to “BIM_LakeCharles_Full.csv” (Fig. 3.8.1.3). Specifying the building IDs that you would like to include in the simulation.

    Fig. 3.8.1.3 R2D ASD setup.¶

  4. Setting the Regional Mapping and SimCenterEvent in the HTA panel (e.g., Fig. 3.8.1.4) where the Event Units should be “Feet” and the Event Type should be “Hurricane”.

    Fig. 3.8.1.4 R2D HTA setup.¶

  5. Setting the “Building Modeling” in MOD panel to “None”.

    Fig. 3.8.1.5 R2D MOD setup.¶

  6. Setting the “Building Analysis Engine” in ANA panel to “IMasEDP”.

    Fig. 3.8.1.6 R2D ANA setup.¶

  7. Setting the “Damage and Loss Method” in DL panel to “HAZUS MH HU”. Downloading the rulset script from DesignSafe PRJ-3207 and setting the Auto populate script to “auto_HU_LA.py” (Fig. 3.8.1.7).

    Fig. 3.8.1.7 R2D DL setup.¶

  8. Setting the “UQ Application” in UQ panel to “None”.

    Fig. 3.8.1.8 R2D UQ setup.¶

After setting up the simulation, please click the RUN to execute the analysis. Once the simulation completed, the app would direct you to the RES panel (Fig. 3.8.1.9) where you could examine and export the results.

Fig. 3.8.1.9 R2D RES panel.¶

3.8.2. Regional Results (NSI-Based Year Built)¶

The geospatial distribution of estimated wind damage states and losses under Hurricane Laura are shown in Fig. 3.8.2.1 (a) and (b), respectively. Referring to Fig. 3.8.2.1 (c), most of the buildings in the studied region (85%) have relatively low to moderate damage (expected Damage State less than 2.0) due to the wind hazard. Referring to Fig. 3.8.2.1 (c), about 20% buildings would expected damage states lower than DS-1 and only about 5% buildings would expect to have damage states exceeding DS-3. The CDF of resulting loss ratios is shown in Fig. 3.8.2.1 (d) where about 40% buildings would expect a loss less than 10% of the total reconstruction cost, and about 30% buildings could see a loss more than 20% of the total reconstruction cost. These results can be accessed in the DesignSafe project, along with the Jupyter notebook used to visualize the results.
